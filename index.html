Kedarnath: The Shrine of Lord Shiva

The Kedarnath Temple is a revered Hindu shrine in the Garhwal Himalayas, dedicated to Lord Shiva and one of the twelve Jyotirlingas. Located in Uttarakhand, India, at an altitude of 3,583 meters, it is a significant pilgrimage site as part of the Chota Char Dham Yatra. The temple's ancient, stone-built architecture houses a conical rock formation representing Shiva in his Sadashiva form.

Yamunotri: Source of the Yamuna River

Yamunotri is a holy place in the mountains of Uttarakhand, India. It is the starting point of the Yamuna River and is one of the four important pilgrimage spots called the Chota Char Dham. The main temple here is dedicated to Goddess Yamuna. It sits high up in the Himalayas, surrounded by beautiful snow-covered peaks. People visit Yamunotri to pray, enjoy the peaceful nature, and feel close to the divine.

Gangotri: Birthplace of the Ganga River

Gangotri is a holy town in the mountains of Uttarakhand, India. It is the place where the river Bhagirathi starts, which later becomes the Ganga River. Gangotri is part of the Chota Char Dham pilgrimage and has a temple dedicated to Goddess Ganga. The temple is built near a special rock called Bhagirath Shila. The town is surrounded by tall mountains, snowy peaks, and green forests. Nearby, the Gangotri Glacier is the source of the river. People visit Gangotri to pray, enjoy nature, and feel close to the divine.
